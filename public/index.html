Vehicle Value Comparison

Choosing the best value in a car is hard. New cars are relatively easy to compare to each other, but what if a used car would be a better value? How would you know?

Cost Per Mile

Enter Cost Per Mile. The Cost Per Mile is an objective score given to a vehicle based on its variable factors. Variable factors include:

  • Purchase price
  • Fuel efficiency
  • Miles on odometer

Other variables are also factored in, but are not vehicle specific:

  • City vs highway driving
  • Fuel cost
  • Low maintenance life (This is the number of miles you expect to get out of a car without any large scale repair.)

Of course there are other costs in car ownership like routine maintenance and insurance. Usually, these costs won't vary much from car to car (within reason) and are therefore not calculated. If you try to compare extremes like a used Pinto and a new Ferrari, sure, the insurance costs will be radically different. But it will still pale compared to the purchase price difference. So we feel pretty good about Cost Per Mile as a reasonable measuring tool.

Give It A Try!

Adjust Settings

It's pretty easy. First, use the Settings to set: Car Life In Miles The number of miles you expect the car to go without major repair. These days, the 200,000 mark seems pretty reasonable. If you're not comfortable with that, make it less. This setting makes comparison between new and used cars possible.

Gas Price

What a gallon of fuel costs.

City/Highway Driving Ratio

The relative amount of miles you'll spend driving in the city and on the highway.

Add Vehicles

Next use the Add Vehicle form to enter information about each car you want to compare.

Year

The model year of the car.

Make

The manufacturer of the car (e.g. Toyota, Dodge, Honda, etc.)

Model

The specific model of the car (e.g. Camry, Civic, etc.)

Odometer Reading

The number of miles currently on the car. New cars can be set to zero, but used cars should be fairly accurate.

City MPG

Miles per gallon efficiency of car during city driving. This can be sourced from car research sites such as Edmunds.com or shared community info sites like FuelEconomy.gov.

Highway MPG

Miles per gallon efficiency on the highway.

Price

What you'll spend to get this car. If you are financing the vehicle, add in the accumulated finance costs such as interest.

Watch Your List Sprout

As you enter car information in, they will be sorted according to their calculated Cost Per Mile. Think gas prices might go up or down? Maybe you'll make the car last longer? Just change the settings and the Cost Per Mile will recalculate and your cars will automatically re-sort!

Enjoy!
